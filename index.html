Introduction

Welcome to the Marketing Work Order System

This User Manual provides comprehensive guidance on how to request, process, and track marketing materials within Revival Research Institute's Patient Recruitment Department. Our standardized work order system ensures consistent quality, efficient workflows, and proper approvals for all marketing deliverables.

Purpose of This Manual

This manual serves as the practical guide for all team members who interact with the Marketing Work Order System, including:

  • Regulatory Affairs Teams - Initiating work orders for new study protocols
  • Clinical Teams - Requesting marketing support for studies
  • Clinical Research Coordinators (CRCs) - Requesting study-specific materials
  • Marketing Team - Creating and managing deliverables
  • Recruitment Team - Developing materials and webpages
  • Corporate Staff - Requesting organizational materials
  • Management - Reviewing and approving materials

How to Use This Manual

  1. Identify Your Need - Determine which type of marketing material you need
  2. Select the Correct SOP - Follow the appropriate Standard Operating Procedure (SOP-01, SOP-02, or SOP-03)
  3. Submit Your Request - Complete the Marketing Work Order Form with all required information
  4. Track Progress - Use Microsoft Planner to monitor your request in real-time
  5. Provide Feedback - Review drafts and provide timely feedback when requested

Key Principles

Clarity

Provide complete and clear specifications to ensure accurate deliverables

Efficiency

Use standardized processes to streamline workflows and reduce turnaround times

Quality

All materials undergo proper review and approval processes

Accountability

Every work order is tracked and documented for complete transparency

System Overview

Three Types of Work Orders

The Marketing Work Order System is organized into three distinct Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs), each designed for specific types of requests:

SOP-01: New Study Protocol Work Order

Purpose: Creating social media lead generation posts for newly initiated clinical trials

Initiated By: Anyone including Regulatory team, clinical teams, or admin

Deliverable: Two (2) social media lead generation posts per study

Approvals Required: Marketing Team Lead Sponsor IRB

Timeline: 7-10 business days (excluding sponsor and IRB approval times)

SOP-02: CRC-Specific Work Order

Purpose: Creating study-specific marketing materials requested by Clinical Research Coordinators

Initiated By: CRCs at individual study sites

Deliverables: Printed brochures and flyers, banners and standees, Pocket Cards and Inclusion & Exclusion Criteria Documents, event-specific promotional materials, digital assets for local promotion

Approvals Required: Recruitment Team Lead CRC US Clinical Teams Sponsor/IRB (only if study-specific and patient-facing)

Timeline: 7-11 business days (varies by complexity)

SOP-03: Non-Study Specific Work Order

Purpose: Creating organizational and corporate marketing materials

Initiated By: Any corporate staff or team member

Deliverables: Business cards, corporate brochures, event materials, internal communications, branded materials

Approvals Required: Recruitment Team Lead Upper Management (if external-facing)

Timeline: 5-10 business days (varies by complexity)

Approval Hierarchy

Material Type Approval Source Timeline
Social Media Lead Generation Posts Sponsor IRB Varies by organization
Study-Specific Patient-Facing Materials US Clinical Teams Sponsor/IRB 2-5 business days (internal)
External Corporate Materials Upper Management 3-5 business days
Internal Draft Content Marketing/Recruitment Team Lead 1-2 business days

The Work Order Process

Standard Workflow for All Work Orders:

  1. Submission - Requester completes Marketing Work Order Form
  2. Review - Team Lead reviews feasibility and assigns resources
  3. Planning - Task created in Microsoft Planner with requester as member
  4. Development - Materials created according to specifications
  5. Internal Review - Quality check by Team Lead
  6. Requester Feedback - Draft shared for feedback (when applicable)
  7. Approvals - Required approvals obtained based on material type
  8. Delivery - Final materials delivered to requester
  9. Closure - Task marked complete in Planner
Key Benefit: Real-Time Tracking

All work orders are managed through Microsoft Planner, giving requesters and stakeholders full visibility into progress, updates, and timeline. You'll receive an email with your Planner task link upon submission, allowing you to track your request from start to finish.

SOP-01: New Study Protocol Work Order

Purpose

SOP-01 defines the standardized process for developing and submitting social media lead generation posts and marketing proposals for new study protocols. This process ensures timely preparation of sponsor-ready marketing materials that support patient recruitment goals.

When to Use SOP-01

Use SOP-01 when:
  • A new study protocol has been received
  • You need social media lead generation posts for patient recruitment
  • Materials require sponsor and IRB approval
Important Note

SOP-01 focuses EXCLUSIVELY on creating two (2) social media lead generation posts per study. Additional marketing materials (flyers, brochures, pocket cards, etc.) are handled through SOP-02: CRC-Specific Work Orders.

Who Can Initiate This Work Order?

The marketing work order can be initiated by anyone including the Regulatory team, clinical teams, or anyone from admin.

Complete Workflow: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Work Order Initiation

Action: Initiator (Regulatory, clinical teams, or admin) submits Marketing Work Order Form upon receipt of new protocol

Information Required:

  • Study name and protocol number
  • Therapeutic area
  • Target population and eligibility criteria
  • Timeline and urgency
  • Any specific messaging requirements
Step 2: Order Routing

Action: Work order automatically routed to Marketing Team

Timeline: Immediate upon submission

Step 3: Material Assessment (Decision Point)

Decision: Marketing Team checks: Do we have existing posts for this therapeutic area?

YES - Existing Posts Available

Relevant posts are identified from the content library and prepared for submission. Posts may be adapted to align with specific study details.

NO - New Posts Required

Marketing Team creates two (2) new lead generation posts aligned with Revival's branding guidelines and the study's target population.

Step 4: New Therapeutic Area Check (Decision Point)

Decision: Is this a new therapeutic area requiring a webpage?

Responsible Team: This assessment and any resulting webpage creation is handled by the Recruitment Team

YES - New Therapeutic Area

Parallel Task: Recruitment Team creates a study webpage with QR code integration. This includes:

  • Dedicated study webpage design and development
  • QR code generation linking to the new webpage
  • SEO optimization
  • Integration with RealTime-CTMS for lead capture

Note: This runs in parallel with material development and does not delay the main workflow.

NO - Existing Therapeutic Area

Process proceeds directly to proposal development without additional webpage creation. Existing webpages and QR codes are utilized.

Step 5: Proposal Development

Action: Marketing Team compiles posts into formal proposal document

Proposal Components:

  • Study overview and target population
  • Two (2) social media lead generation posts (creative + copy)
  • Proposed targeting strategy (demographics, geography, interests)
  • Performance metrics and tracking plan
  • QR codes linking to study webpage
Step 6: Sponsor Submission

Action: Executive Assistant Team submits proposal to Sponsor for approval

Submitted Package Includes:

  • Formal marketing proposal document
  • Visual mockups of social media posts
  • Copy for each post
  • Targeting strategy
Step 7: Sponsor Approval

Action: Sponsor reviews and approves marketing materials

Timeline: Varies by sponsor organization (typically 3-7 business days)

Possible Outcomes:

  • Approved as submitted
  • Approved with minor modifications
  • Requires revision and resubmission
Step 8: IRB Submission

Action: Regulatory Affairs submits approved materials to IRB

Responsible Party: Regulatory Affairs team

Timeline: Varies by IRB (typically 5-10 business days)

Step 9: Planner Closure

Action: Task marked complete in Microsoft Planner upon IRB approval

Final Steps:

  • All approvals documented and stored on OneDrive
  • Marketing materials ready for deployment
  • Stakeholders notified of completion

Timeline Summary

Standard Turnaround Time: 7-10 Business Days

From work order submission to sponsor submission (excluding sponsor and IRB approval times, which vary by organization)

Breakdown:

  • Initial review and routing: 1 business day
  • Content development or selection: 2-4 business days
  • Internal review and refinement: 1-2 business days
  • Proposal compilation: 1 business day
  • Executive Assistant submission: 1-2 business days

Records & Documentation

The following records are maintained for each SOP-01 work order:

  • Marketing Work Order Form - Initial request documentation
  • Study Protocol - Reference document for content development
  • Social Media Lead Generation Posts - Two finalized posts with creative and copy
  • Marketing Proposal Document - Comprehensive proposal submitted to sponsor
  • Sponsor Approval Communication - Email or letter confirming sponsor approval
  • IRB Approval Documentation - Final authorization for material use
  • Microsoft Planner Task Log - Complete activity tracking and timeline
  • OneDrive Storage Link - Central repository for all materials and approvals

SOP-02: CRC-Specific Work Order

Purpose

SOP-02 outlines the standardized process for Clinical Research Coordinators (CRCs) to request study-specific marketing materials or recruitment support. This ensures that materials are created on an as-needed basis based on actual site requirements, preventing waste and maximizing resource efficiency.

When to Use SOP-02

Use SOP-02 when:
  • You are a CRC needing study-specific marketing materials
  • Your site requires printed materials or promotional items
  • You need materials for local events or community outreach
  • Additional marketing support is needed beyond the standard social media posts (created via SOP-01)
Key Difference from SOP-01

While SOP-01 focuses on creating social media posts automatically for every new study, SOP-02 creates additional materials only when CRCs specifically request them. This prevents overproduction of materials that may not be utilized.

Available Materials

CRCs can request the following types of materials through SOP-02:

  • Printed brochures and flyers
  • Banners and standees
  • Pocket Cards and Inclusion & Exclusion Criteria Documents
  • Event-specific promotional materials
  • Digital assets for local promotion

Complete Workflow: Step-by-Step

Step 1: CRC Submits Work Order

Action: CRC completes Marketing Work Order Form specifying study name, materials needed, and deadline

Information Required:

  • Study name and protocol number
  • Specific materials needed (type, quantity, size)
  • Purpose and use case (e.g., community event, physician outreach)
  • Required deadline
  • Any special requirements or preferences
  • Delivery address and contact information
Step 2: Recruitment Team Review

Action: Recruitment Team Lead reviews feasibility and assigns task in Planner

Assessment Criteria:

  • Clarity and completeness of request
  • Resource requirements and team capacity
  • Timeline feasibility
  • Need for additional information or clarification

If additional information is needed, Team Lead contacts CRC for clarification before proceeding.

Step 3: Planner Link Shared

Action: CRC receives email with Planner link for transparency and real-time tracking

Benefits of Planner Access:

  • Track progress in real-time
  • View updates and comments from the team
  • Communicate directly within the task
  • Access shared files and drafts
  • Receive notifications on status changes
Step 4: New Therapeutic Area Check (Decision Point)

Decision: Is this a new therapeutic area requiring a webpage?

Responsible Team: Recruitment Team handles this assessment and any webpage creation

YES - New Therapeutic Area

Parallel Task: Recruitment Team creates study webpage with QR code integration

  • Dedicated webpage designed and developed
  • QR codes generated for print materials
  • Webpage optimized for SEO and mobile devices
  • Integrated with RealTime-CTMS for lead capture

This runs parallel to material development.

NO - Existing Therapeutic Area

Proceeds directly to material development using existing webpages and QR codes.

Step 5: Material Development

Action: Recruitment Team creates requested materials with brand compliance

Development Standards:

  • Design adheres to Revival's branding guidelines
  • Content is medically accurate and compliant
  • QR codes integrated linking to study webpage
  • All materials include necessary legal disclaimers
  • Contact information and study details accurately represented
  • Print specifications meet production requirements (resolution, bleed, color mode)
Step 6: Internal Review

Action: Materials reviewed internally by Recruitment Team for quality and compliance

Review Checklist:

  • Language clarity and effectiveness
  • Brand alignment and visual consistency
  • Technical accuracy (QR codes, contact info, study details)
  • Print-readiness or digital optimization
  • Compliance with advertising standards
Step 7: CRC Review & Approval

Action: Draft materials shared with requesting CRC for feedback and approval

CRC Evaluates:

  • Relevance to site needs and local context
  • Accuracy of study information
  • Suitability for intended use
  • Any necessary modifications or adjustments

Revisions are made based on CRC feedback before proceeding to clinical review.

Step 8: Clinical Team Review

Action: Materials sent to US Clinical Teams for medical accuracy and compliance review

Clinical Team Reviews For:

  • Medical information accuracy
  • Appropriate disease descriptions
  • Correct representation of study procedures
  • Compliance with FDA guidelines for clinical trial advertising
  • Inclusion of all required disclaimers
  • No prohibited claims or guarantees
Step 9: Sponsor and/or IRB Submission

Action: Sponsor and/or IRB submission only if they are study-specific materials that may be patient-facing.

When Required:

  • Materials will be distributed directly to potential participants
  • Materials contain study-specific recruitment messaging
  • Materials make claims about the study or condition

Not Required For:

  • Internal tools (pocket cards for CRC use only)
  • Non-patient-facing materials
  • Generic organizational materials
Step 10: Final Delivery

Action: Approved materials delivered to CRC for implementation

Delivery Methods:

  • Digital files shared via OneDrive or email
  • Print materials shipped to site address
  • Large format items coordinated for pickup or delivery
Step 11: Task Closure

Action: Task marked complete in Planner after CRC acknowledgment

Final Documentation:

  • All approvals saved on OneDrive
  • Final materials archived
  • CRC confirms receipt and satisfaction
  • Task closed in Microsoft Planner

Timeline Summary

Standard Turnaround Time: 7-11 Business Days

Timeline varies based on:

  • Complexity of materials requested
  • Number of revisions required
  • Whether a new webpage is needed
  • Approval turnaround times from CRC and clinical teams
  • Sponsor/IRB requirements (if applicable)

Rush requests may be accommodated when feasible with advance notice.

Records & Documentation

The following records are maintained for each SOP-02 work order:

  • Marketing Work Order Form - Initial CRC request
  • Draft Materials - All versions with revision history
  • CRC Approval Communication - Feedback and sign-off from requesting CRC
  • Clinical Team Review Notes - Medical accuracy and compliance approval
  • Sponsor/IRB Approval (if applicable) - External approval documentation
  • Final Materials - Approved versions ready for use
  • Delivery Confirmation - CRC acknowledgment of receipt
  • Microsoft Planner Task Log - Complete activity and communication history
  • OneDrive Storage - Centralized repository for all files

SOP-03: Non-Study Specific Work Order

Purpose

SOP-03 establishes a standardized process for requesting and developing non-study-specific marketing materials required for organizational, corporate, and general operational purposes. These materials support Revival Research Institute's brand presence, employee engagement, community outreach, and corporate communications.

When to Use SOP-03

Use SOP-03 when you need:
  • Corporate identity materials (business cards, letterheads, templates)
  • Event and community outreach materials (banners, booth displays)

Key Distinction

Non-study specific materials do NOT require Sponsor approval or IRB submission. However, materials representing the organization externally must receive Upper Management approval to ensure brand consistency and appropriate messaging.

Program & Initiative Materials

    Definitions & Key Terms

    This section provides definitions for key terms used throughout the Marketing Work Order System.

    System & Process Terms

    Marketing Work Order

    A formal request submitted through the standardized form for marketing materials or recruitment support. Work orders are categorized into three types (SOP-01, SOP-02, SOP-03) based on the nature of the request.

    Microsoft Planner

    The project management and task tracking tool used to manage all marketing work orders. Planner provides real-time visibility, collaboration features, file sharing, and complete audit trails for all activities.

    OneDrive

    The centralized cloud storage repository where all marketing materials, drafts, approvals, and final deliverables are stored. OneDrive ensures version control, accessibility, and secure file management.

    RealTime-CTMS

    Revival's Clinical Trial Management System that captures and manages participant leads. Study webpages integrate with RealTime-CTMS to enable seamless lead capture and tracking.

    Material Types

    Social Media Lead Generation Posts

    Paid advertisements designed for social media platforms (Facebook, Instagram, etc.) intended to generate interest and capture leads for clinical trial recruitment. These posts include both creative (visual design) and copy (text messaging).

    Marketing Proposal

    A formal document submitted to study Sponsors that includes social media posts, targeting strategy, performance metrics, and campaign overview. Proposals require sponsor approval before implementation.

    Pocket Cards

    Small, wallet-sized reference cards that contain study eligibility criteria and contact information. Primarily used by CRCs as quick-reference tools.

    Inclusion & Exclusion Criteria Documents

    Detailed documents listing the specific criteria that determine who can (inclusion) and cannot (exclusion) participate in a clinical study. These are essential tools for CRCs and recruitment staff.

    Study Webpage

    A dedicated landing page on Revival's website that provides information about a specific study or therapeutic area. Webpages are optimized for search engines (SEO) and integrate with RealTime-CTMS for lead capture. QR codes on printed materials link to these webpages.

    QR Code

    Quick Response code - a scannable barcode that links directly to a study webpage. QR codes are integrated into all printed recruitment materials to provide easy digital access for potential participants.

    Patient-Facing Materials

    Marketing materials that will be viewed or received by potential study participants or the general public. These materials require more stringent review and approval processes, including clinical review and often Sponsor/IRB approval.

    Approval & Review Terms

    Internal Review

    The first-level quality check conducted by the Marketing Team Lead or Recruitment Team Lead to ensure brand alignment, technical accuracy, and professional quality before materials are shared externally.

    Clinical Review

    Medical accuracy and compliance review conducted by US Clinical Teams to ensure materials are medically accurate, compliant with FDA guidelines, and include all required disclaimers.

    Sponsor Approval

    Formal approval from the pharmaceutical company or organization sponsoring a clinical trial. Sponsors review marketing materials to ensure accuracy, appropriate messaging, and alignment with their brand and regulatory requirements.

    IRB (Institutional Review Board)

    An independent ethics committee that reviews and approves research involving human participants. Marketing materials for clinical trials must receive IRB approval to ensure they meet ethical standards and regulatory requirements.

    Upper Management Approval

    Strategic review and approval by Revival's leadership for materials that represent the organization externally. This ensures consistency with corporate strategy, values, and professional standards.

    Therapeutic Area & Study Terms

    Therapeutic Area

    A medical specialty or category of diseases/conditions (e.g., cardiology, oncology, neurology, dermatology). Studies are organized by therapeutic area, and this categorization determines whether a new webpage is needed.

    New Therapeutic Area

    A therapeutic area that Revival has not previously worked in or does not currently have an active study webpage for. New therapeutic areas trigger the creation of a dedicated webpage and associated QR codes.

    Study Protocol

    The comprehensive document provided by the study Sponsor that outlines all details of the clinical trial, including objectives, design, methodology, eligibility criteria, and procedures. The protocol serves as the foundation for all marketing content development.

    CRC (Clinical Research Coordinator)

    Study site staff member responsible for day-to-day management of clinical trials, including participant recruitment, screening, enrollment, and follow-up. CRCs are the primary requesters of site-specific marketing materials.

    Branding & Design Terms

    Brand Guidelines

    Revival Research Institute's official standards for visual identity, including logo usage, color palette, typography, imagery style, and messaging tone. All marketing materials must adhere to these guidelines.

    Print Specifications

    Technical requirements for printed materials, including resolution (300 DPI), color mode (CMYK), bleed area, trim size, and file format. Proper specifications ensure high-quality printed output.

    Digital Optimization

    The process of preparing digital materials for optimal performance online, including appropriate file formats, compressed file sizes, web-safe colors, and responsive design for mobile devices.

    SEO (Search Engine Optimization)

    Techniques used to improve a webpage's visibility in search engine results. Study webpages are optimized with relevant keywords, meta descriptions, and structured content to help potential participants find studies through search engines.

    Common Questions & Answers

    Q: How do I submit a Marketing Work Order?

    A: Complete the Marketing Work Order Form (available on the shared drive or from your team lead). Include all required information and supporting documents. The form will be automatically routed to the appropriate team.

    Q: Can I request rush or expedited service?

    A: Yes, rush requests may be accommodated when feasible. Indicate "RUSH" in the subject line and provide justification for the urgency. Note that rush requests depend on team capacity and may require approval from the team lead.

    Q: How will I know the status of my work order?

    A: You'll receive an email with a Microsoft Planner link upon submission. You can track real-time progress, view updates, access drafts, and communicate with the team directly through Planner.

    Q: What if I need to make changes after submitting my work order?

    A: Contact the team lead or comment in the Planner task as soon as possible. Changes can usually be accommodated if the work hasn't progressed too far. Significant changes may impact the timeline.

    Q: Do all materials need Sponsor and IRB approval?

    A: No. Only materials created through SOP-01 (social media posts) always require Sponsor and IRB approval. SOP-02 materials require Sponsor/IRB approval only if they are study-specific and patient-facing. SOP-03 materials never require Sponsor/IRB approval but may need Upper Management approval if external-facing.

    Q: How many revisions are included?

    A: Reasonable revisions based on feedback are included in the standard process. However, frequent or extensive revisions may impact timelines. Provide clear, complete feedback to minimize revision cycles.

    Q: Can I request materials for multiple studies at once?

    A: Yes, but submit a separate work order for each study to ensure proper tracking and accountability. This allows each request to be managed independently with its own timeline and approvals.

    Q: What file formats will I receive?

    A: Digital materials are typically delivered as PDFs, PNGs, or JPGs. Print materials include print-ready PDFs with bleed and crop marks. Source files (Adobe InDesign, Photoshop, etc.) can be provided upon request.

    Q: Who do I contact if I have questions?

    A: Contact your team lead or comment directly in the Microsoft Planner task associated with your work order. For general system questions, reach out to the Recruitment Department Lead.

    Best Practices for Requesters

    To ensure smooth processing and high-quality deliverables:

    Be Comprehensive

    Provide all necessary information upfront. Include study protocols, brand guidelines, preferred imagery, size specifications, quantity requirements, and any special considerations.

    Be Clear

    Write clear, specific descriptions of what you need. Vague requests lead to multiple revision cycles. If possible, include examples or reference materials.

    Be Realistic

    Allow adequate time for material development and approval processes. Standard timelines exist for a reason - rushing often compromises quality.

    Be Responsive

    Respond promptly to requests for feedback or clarification. Delays in your response create delays in delivery. Check Planner regularly for updates.

    Be Collaborative

    Trust the expertise of the marketing and recruitment teams. They bring professional knowledge of design, messaging, and compliance. Be open to their suggestions.

    Be Organized

    Keep your supporting documents organized and accessible. Use clear file names and provide context for any materials you share.
